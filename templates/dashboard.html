About the Indian Constitution

The Constitution of India is the supreme law of the land. It lays down the framework defining fundamental political principles, powers of government, and citizens' rights and duties.

History

Drafted by the Constituent Assembly, chaired by Dr. B.R. Ambedkar, the Constitution came into effect on 26 January 1950. It replaced the Government of India Act (1935) and established India as a sovereign republic.
